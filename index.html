I'm a Network Engineering Student and minimal web developer.
My interests span across networking, cybersecurity, and building clean, simple things for the web.
I'm drawn to how systems connect, how they break, and how to build things that just work.


I first got into this through pure curiosity — Online resources, YouTube, and a lot of free time. I started with HTML and CSS, picked up some Photoshop, then fell into Linux and never really left. That rabbit hole eventually led me to security tools, Wireshark, Burp Suite, Kali Linux, and a fascination with how the internet works under the hood. Now I study Network Engineering, because after all of it, I kept coming back to the same thing — you have to understand how systems work before you can build or break anything meaningful.


This is my little corner of the internet — where I share my projects, photos, and thoughts.
I write occasionally about tech, networking, online security, and the books and ideas that stick with me.
